FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common apostille and notarization questions.

An apostille is an official government certification that authenticates a document for use in foreign countries that are members of the Hague Apostille Convention. It verifies the authenticity of the signature, seal, or stamp — not the document's content — so it will be legally recognized abroad.

If your destination country is a Hague Convention member, you need an apostille. If the country is not a member, you need embassy or consulate legalization — a multi-step process. We can confirm which process applies to your situation before you submit.

Remote Online Notarization (RON) allows documents to be notarized through a secure video session — no in-person appointment required. You can complete the process from anywhere in the US using a computer or smartphone.
