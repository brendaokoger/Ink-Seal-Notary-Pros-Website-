Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about apostille services, remote online notarization, international document authentication, and more.

An apostille is a form of international document authentication used for countries that are part of the Hague Apostille Convention. It verifies the authenticity of signatures, seals, and stamps on public documents so they can be recognized abroad.

Countries that are members of the Hague Apostille Convention accept apostilles. These countries recognize apostilled documents without requiring additional embassy or consulate legalization.

Hague Convention countries are nations that participate in the Hague Apostille Convention treaty. This agreement simplifies the international document authentication process between member countries.

Processing times vary depending on the state, document type, and whether expedited service is requested. Most apostille requests are completed within several business days, while some services may be completed sooner with rush processing.

Remote Online Notarization (RON) allows documents to be notarized electronically through a secure online video session. Signers can complete notarizations remotely from the comfort of their home or office.

The required process depends on the destination country and document type. Countries within the Hague Convention typically require an apostille, while non-member countries often require embassy or consulate legalization.
